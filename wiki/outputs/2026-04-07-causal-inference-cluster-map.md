# 2026-04-07 Causal Inference Cluster Map

## Overview

The `Causal Inference` and `Causality` folders add an epistemic and methodological layer that the rest of the repo was missing. They focus on identification, intervention, latent structure, time-series causality, and the bridge between causal reasoning and machine learning.

## Main Threads

- **Foundations and core texts**: [Introduction to Causal Inference](../sources/introduction-to-causal-inference.md), [Elements of Causal Inference](../sources/elements-of-causal-inference.md), and [The Mathematics of Causal Inference](../sources/the-mathematics-of-causal-inference.md) anchor the foundational layer.
- **Causality for ML**: [Causality for Machine Learning](../sources/causality-for-machine-learning.md), [Causal Analysis and ML](../sources/causal-analysis-and-ml.md), and [Causal Machine Learning](../sources/causal-machine-learning.md) connect causal ideas to modern modeling practice.
- **Identification and instrumental variables**: [Instrumental Variable Methods for Causal Inference](../sources/instrumental-variable-methods-for-causal-inference.md) and [A Review of IV Estimators in Mendalian Randomization](../sources/a-review-of-iv-estimators-in-mendalian-randomization.md) anchor the identification thread.
- **Latents, time, and structure**: [Causal Reasoning in the Presence of Latent](../sources/causal-reasoning-in-the-presence-of-latent.md), [Causal Inference for Time series Analysis](../sources/causal-inference-for-time-series-analysis.md), and [Problems of Statistical Estimation and Causal Inference in Time-Series Regression](../sources/problems-of-statistical-estimation-and-causal-inference-in-time-series-regression.md) extend the cluster beyond simple static settings.
- **Causal representations**: [Toward Causal Representation Learning](../sources/toward-causal-representation-learning.md) makes this cluster relevant to representation learning and model abstraction.

## Current Read

- This cluster upgrades the repo’s notion of explanation by distinguishing causal explanation from predictive success.
- It is one of the best complements to the LLM/agents work because it asks what constitutes valid inference rather than only stronger performance.
- The most reusable conceptual spine is causal inference, identification, and causal representation learning.
