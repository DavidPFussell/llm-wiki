# 2026-04-07 Language Models Cluster Map

## Overview

The `Language Models` folder extends the existing `LLM` cluster backward and outward. It adds more historical language-modeling lineage, more architecture-focused papers, and several bridge papers between classic statistical language modeling and modern transformer-era systems.

## Main Threads

- **Model families and scaling**: [LaMDA](../sources/lamda.md), [GATO](../sources/gato.md), [A Roadmap for Big Model](../sources/a-roadmap-for-big-model.md), and [Recent Advances in NLP via Large Pre-trained Language Models](../sources/recent-advances-in-nlp-via-large-pre-trained-language-models.md) reinforce the landscape/model-family thread.
- **Architecture and long-context design**: [Longformer](../sources/longformer.md), [An Attention Free Transformer](../sources/an-attention-free-transformer.md), and [Formal Algorithms for Transformers](../sources/formal-algorithms-for-transformers.md) strengthen the architecture side of the corpus.
- **Reasoning and prompt-era capability**: [Chain of Thought Prompting](../sources/chain-of-thought-prompting.md), [On the Advance of Making Language Models Better Reasoners](../sources/on-the-advance-of-making-language-models-better-reasoners.md), and [Solving Quantitative Reasoning Problems with Language Models](../sources/solving-quantitative-reasoning-problems-with-language-models.md) connect this folder directly to reasoning-oriented work.
- **Knowledge and document structure**: [Language Models are Open Knowledge Graphs](../sources/language-models-are-open-knowledge-graphs.md), [DocBert](../sources/docbert.md), and [From Information to Knowledge](../sources/from-information-to-knowledge-harvesting-entities-and-relationships-from-web-sources.md) point toward structured knowledge and document modeling.
- **Pre-transformer language-modeling lineage**: [SRILM](../sources/srilm-an-extensible-language-modeling-kit.md), [KenLM](../sources/kenlm-faster-and-smaller-language-model-queries.md), [N-gram Language Model Smoothing](../sources/n-gram-language-model-smoothing.md), and related sources make this folder the strongest historical language-modeling bridge in the current wiki.

## Current Read

- This folder is not just “more LLM papers.” It broadens the corpus from modern application-facing LLM work toward the technical lineage underneath it.
- It also strengthens the wiki’s ability to compare transformer-era intuitions against older statistical language-modeling practice.
- The most reusable anchors here are architecture, in-context learning, and the historical transition from n-gram systems to large pretrained models.
