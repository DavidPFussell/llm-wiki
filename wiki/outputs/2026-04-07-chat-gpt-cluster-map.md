# 2026-04-07 Chat-GPT Cluster Map

## Overview

The `Chat-GPT` folder is the most application-facing and prompt-heavy part of the current LLM wave. It mixes papers, reports, prompt guides, evaluation work, and reasoning-scaffold methods around the broad cultural and technical emergence of chat-native LLMs.

## Main Threads

- **Prompting and reasoning scaffolds**: [Graph of Thoughts](../sources/graph-of-thoughts.md), [Meta-Prompting](../sources/meta-prompting-enhancing-language-models-with-task-agnostic-scaffolding.md), [Skeleton of Thought](../sources/skeleton-of-thought.md), and [Enhancing Zero-Shot Chain-of-Thought Reasoning](../sources/enhancing-zero-shot-chain-of-thought-reasoning-in-large.md) deepen the prompt-design side of the wiki.
- **Reliability and hallucination control**: [Chain-of-Verification](../sources/chain-of-verification-reduces-hallucination-in-large-language-models.md), [SelfCheckGPT](../sources/selfcheckgpt.md), and [Retrieval Meets Long Context LLMs](../sources/retrieval-meets-long-context-llms.md) reinforce the reliability thread.
- **Instruction tuning and synthetic supervision**: [Self-Instruct](../sources/self-instruct.md), [ORCA](../sources/orca.md), [Orca - Progressive Learning from Complex Explanation Traces of GPT-4](../sources/orca-progressive-learning-from-complex-explanation-traces-of-gpt-4.md), and [Principled Instructions Are All You Need](../sources/principled-instructions-are-all-you-need.md) cluster around instruction following and synthetic data.
- **Evaluation and practical use**: [AgentBench](../sources/agentbench-evaluating-llms-as-agents.md), [ChatGPT Outperforms Mechanical Turk for Text Annotation](../sources/chatgpt-outperforms-mechanical-turk-for-text-annotation.md), and [Is ChatGPT a General Purpose NLP Task Solver](../sources/is-chatgpt-a-general-purpose-nlp-task-solver.md) make this folder especially useful for benchmark and applied-use questions.
- **Structured knowledge and planning**: [Unifying LLMs and KGs](../sources/unifying-llms-and-kgs.md), [DeepOnto](../sources/deeponto.md), and [Language Models as Zero-Short Planners](../sources/language-models-as-zero-short-planners.md) tie chat-oriented papers back to structured reasoning and knowledge representation.

## Current Read

- This folder captures the phase where LLM work became much more operational, user-facing, and scaffold-oriented.
- It is especially relevant to the LLM Wiki project because many of these papers are about external control structure, verification, and practical instruction design.
- The folder also overlaps heavily with the `Agent` cluster, especially on evaluation and reasoning structure.
