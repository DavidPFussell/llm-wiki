# 2026-04-07 Grammar Cluster Map

## Overview

The `Grammar` folder is really a grammar-plus-information-extraction shelf. It combines parsing, open information extraction, relation extraction, ontology learning, and some more foundational linguistic material.

## Main Threads

- **Open information extraction**: [A Survey on Open IR](../sources/a-survey-on-open-ir.md), [Methods for OIE](../sources/methods-for-oie.md), [OIE from Web](../sources/oie-from-web.md), and [Stanford OpenIE](../sources/stanford-openie.md) define the extraction-first branch.
- **Parsing and dependency structure**: [Dependency Parsing](../sources/dependency-parsing.md) and [Universal Stanford Dependencies](../sources/universal-stanford-dependencies.md) provide the syntactic backbone.
- **Relation extraction**: [Distant Supervision for Relation Extraction without Labeled Data](../sources/distant-supervision-for-relation-extraction-without-labeled-data.md) and [Cross-sentence N-ary Relation Extraction with Graph LSTMs](../sources/cross-sentence-n-ary-relation-extraction-with-graph-lstms.md) connect grammar to structured extraction.
- **Ontology and lexical semantics**: [Learning Domain Ontologies](../sources/learning-domain-ontologies.md), [Categories and Foundational Ontology](../sources/categories-and-foundational-ontology.md), and [A Statistical Model for Near-Synonym Choice](../sources/a-statistical-model-for-near-synonym-choice.md) expand the shelf toward semantics.

## Current Read

- This cluster is one of the clearest bridges from classical NLP to knowledge graph construction.
- It should eventually feed entity extraction, relation extraction, and topic/keyphrase workflows in the wiki itself.
