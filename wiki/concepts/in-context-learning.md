# In-Context Learning

## Definition

In-context learning is the ability of language models to adapt behavior from prompts, demonstrations, or examples without updating model weights.

## Current Synthesis

- It is one of the central conceptual pivots between prompting, few-shot learning, and emergent model capabilities.
- It often blurs the line between inference-time scaffolding and learned capability.
- This wiki is likely to revisit it across prompt design, reasoning, and evaluation threads.
