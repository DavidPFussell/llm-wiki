# 2026-04-07 Causality, Clustering, and AI Lint Pass

## Main Findings

- **Causal Inference is already concept-coherent**: even with few entity pages, the cluster has a strong conceptual backbone because the literature is organized more by methods and principles than by named systems.
- **Clustering is method-rich but still light on synthesis**: source coverage is good, but a future comparison note on clustering paradigms would make the cluster much more usable.
- **AI is broad and intentionally fuzzy**: it works best as a framing cluster, not as a tightly scoped topic. It will need selective deepening rather than indiscriminate expansion.
- **Cross-links are becoming more interesting**: causality connects naturally to explanation and scientific reasoning, clustering connects to representation learning and corpus organization, and AI foundations connect to nearly everything else.

## Recommended Next Steps

- Write a comparison note for clustering paradigms and validation strategies.
- Write one synthesis on causality vs interpretability vs explanation.
